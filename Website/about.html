How My Opinion Changed

From a very young age I lsitened to whatever my dad listened to. He was born in the early 70's, so he liked all of the hard rock from the 70's and beyond. When the 90's came around my dad got into North Western Grunge bands like Nirvana and Alice in Chains. From a young age I was listening to this combanation of music: 70's Hard Rock and 90's Grunge. When I got older around my middle school years I found out about Eminem and that sparked my intrest in rap. Also around this time I started getting into 2000's punk rock (similar to the 90's grunge) with bands like Linkin Park and Blink-182. For a long time that's what I listened to. When I got to highschool I started listening to exclusively rap music and grew very fond of the synthetic beats and the deep intellectual lyrics. When I graduated and found more people with an intrest in Rock I went back to my roots and re-deicovered all the songs I hadn't listened to in years. And out of that spawned my intrest in alternative rock with bands like Cage The Elephant.

Douglas Mulka

My name is Doug and I love music. I am currently a college student at Kettering University in Michigan. I decided to build this website so that I can learn about building websites and also as a way to classify all of the types of music that I listen to. It was a very fun task and I feel like I learned a lot doing it.

Over the past 3 months I have developed a more refined taste in music as I was exposed to many new genres and artists by friends that I have made in recent times. As a child, I grew up in New York, so all of my friends at the time were all about East Coast Rap and my dad listened to a lot of classic rock and grunge. Those were the two genres that I remember listening to as a young kid. As I got older I started forming my own tastes and intrests in music. Now looking at my music on my phone I realized that it all fits into 3 categories: Rap, Rock and Pop.
